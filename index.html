about

Growing up, I always had a passion for technology; an outlier in a small town. The only opportunity available to me there to pursue this passion was through retail of electronics. During my time there, I'd like to say I learned quite a bit. Not just about the plethora of hardware, but also about people. Being that we were the only shop of our kind in the town, people with all sorts of problems came to us, regardless of whether it was something we'd dealt with previously or not. And to me, that was the best part of the job. It allowed me to step outside of the shell of a salesperson and apply some actual problem solving. Even though retail wasn't for me, I'd like to think I have that job to thank for allowing me to uncover my love of problem solving. Coupling that with my passion for technology; what better pursuit than software development?

These days I'm wrapping up my diploma program at NAIT for Digital Media & IT. Even after two years and all the languages, frameworks, and approaches I've taken, I know I've just barely scratched the surface. But I've since learned that I was looking at things the wrong way. What could honestly be more exciting than knowing that you still have so much more you can learn? I look forward to all the possibilities the future holds!
